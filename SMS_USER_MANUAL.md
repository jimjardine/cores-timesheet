# Texting In Your Timesheet — Tech Guide

Text your hours to **+1 (506) 404-6969** every work day. No app, no login — just a normal text message. Write it however you'd say it; the system figures it out.

---

## The short version

One text at the end of the day covers everything:

> In 7:30, 4760 6hrs rebuilt port engine bearings, 4862 2hrs fuel lines, out 4, lunch 30, no PD

That's: start time, each job with hours and what you did, finish time, lunch, and whether you're staying overnight. Send that and you're done.

You'll get a confirmation back like:

> Done Joey ✓ Fri Jul 3 | 7:30am–4pm | lunch 30min
> 4760: 6hrs reg
> 4862: 2hrs reg
> Total: 8hrs reg
> No per diem
> Nicki has it.

If the confirmation looks wrong, just text the correction (see below).

---

## What to include

| Thing | How to say it |
|---|---|
| Start time | "In 7:30" |
| Finish time | "Out 4:30" |
| Each job | Job number + hours + what you did: "4760 6hrs rebuilt bearings" |
| Lunch | "lunch 30", "half hour lunch", or "no lunch" |
| Per diem | Where you're staying ("staying at Delta Halifax") or "no PD" |

**Job descriptions matter** — write a few words about what you actually did on each job. That goes on the client's paperwork.

Hours can be decimals: "6.5hrs", "4.25hrs" both work.

Don't stress about typos, caps lock, or run-on sentences — it reads texts the way people actually write them.

---

## You don't have to send it all at once

Text as you go if that's easier:

- Morning: **"In 7:30, 4760 6hrs — engine work"**
- Later: **"4862 2hrs fuel lines"**
- End of day: **"Out 4:30, lunch 30, staying at Delta Halifax"**

It stitches your texts together into one timesheet for the day.

If you leave something out (like lunch or per diem), you'll get **one** quick question back, something like:

> Quick: lunch? ("lunch 30" or "no lunch") | per diem tonight? (location or "no PD")

Answer it and you're done. It won't nag you past that — anything still missing gets flagged for Nicki to sort out.

## Made a mistake? Just text again

Already got your "Done ✓" but forgot a job or got the time wrong? Text the fix — same day, same number:

> "Also 4881 1.5hrs hydraulic hose"
> "Out was actually 5, not 4:30"

New jobs get added and corrected times replace the old ones.

## Forgot to text yesterday?

Say which day it's for and it'll go on the right date:

> "Yesterday: in 8, 4760 8hrs shaft alignment, no lunch, no PD"

Day names ("Tuesday") and dates ("June 30") work too. If you don't mention a day, it assumes today.

## Don't know the job number?

Text **JOBS** and you'll get the list of open jobs with their vessels.

Text **JOBS** plus a boat name to narrow it down:

> "JOBS wave master"

## Using someone else's phone?

Start your message with your name so the hours land on your timesheet, not theirs:

> "This is Joey — in 7, 4760 8hrs gearbox, lunch 30, no PD"

If you text from a number the system doesn't recognize, it'll ask who you are — reply "This is [your name]".

## Worked all day on one job and didn't count hours?

If you give your in and out times, it can work out the hours for you:

> "In 7, out 5, lunch 60, worked on 4760 all day"

## Stuck?

- Text **HELP** (or just **?**) any time for the cheat sheet.
- If a message comes back with "Couldn't read that one," reword it or text Nicki directly.

---

## What happens to your text

Nothing hits payroll automatically. Nicki sees every submission in the review screen, checks it (including anything flagged as missing), and approves it before it counts. Overtime past 8 hours in a day is split out automatically — you'll see it in your confirmation as "reg + OT".

**Bottom line: one text a day, before you forget. If you got the "Done ✓" reply, Nicki has it.**
